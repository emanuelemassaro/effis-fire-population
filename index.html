Geospatial Analysis · Europe · 2019–2023

Population Exposed to
Wildfires in Europe

How many people live in areas burned by wildfires between 2019 and 2023? A spatial analysis combining satellite fire data with the Eurostat Census 2021 population grid — broken down by age group, NUTS2 region, and country.

38,139 fire records 455 M people assessed 334 NUTS2 regions 1 km population grid

Key Findings

Across Europe, roughly 270,000 people lived within areas that burned at least once between 2019 and 2023. Fire perimeters were dissolved into a single layer before the analysis, so individuals living in areas affected by multiple fires are counted only once.

Data Sources

🛰️

EFFIS MODIS Burned Area

Wildfire perimeters detected by the MODIS satellite, provided by the European Forest Fire Information System (EFFIS) at the Joint Research Centre (JRC). 38,139 fire polygons for 2019–2023, in EPSG:4326.

👥

Eurostat Census Grid 2021 V2.2

Population counts on a 1 km × 1 km grid covering the EU and neighbouring countries, projected in EPSG:3035 (LAEA Europe). Variables: total population, under 15, 15–64, and 65+.

🗺️

NUTS2 Boundaries 2021

Administrative regions at NUTS level 2, sourced from Eurostat GISCO. 334 regions covering the EU, EEA, and candidate countries, in EPSG:4326.

Methodology

The analysis is implemented in Python using geopandas, rasterio, and rasterstats. All code is available on GitHub.

  1. Filter fire records (2019–2023)

    Load the EFFIS MODIS shapefile and retain only records whose FIREDATE falls between 2019 and 2023 (38,139 polygons).

  2. Dissolve fire perimeters

    Union all fire polygons into a single MultiPolygon using shapely.ops.unary_union. Areas burned in multiple years are collapsed, so no person is counted more than once.

  3. Intersect with NUTS2 regions

    The dissolved fire extent is intersected with each NUTS2 boundary (reprojected to EPSG:3035) to obtain the burned sub-polygon per region. 251 out of 334 NUTS2 regions overlapped with at least one fire.

  4. Zonal statistics on the Census rasters

    For each age-group raster (1 km grid, EPSG:3035, nodata = −9999), rasterstats.zonal_stats sums grid cells whose centres fall inside (a) the fire intersection per NUTS2 and (b) the full NUTS2 boundary. This gives exposed and total population per region.

  5. Aggregate to country level

    NUTS2 results are grouped by CNTR_CODE to produce national totals and percentages.

⚠️ Limitations: Population data are from the 2021 Census and are held constant across all fire years. The analysis measures the residential population living within burned perimeters — it does not account for seasonal residents, evacuations, casualties, or smoke exposure beyond the burned area.
